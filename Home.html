Instant tips to various medical Emergencies


1.) Heart Attack

Symptoms: Symptoms of a heart attack can vary from person to person. They may be mild or severe. Women, older adults, and people with diabetes are more likely to have subtle or unusual symptoms. Symptons in adult may include:Changes in mental status, especially in older adults.Chest pain that feels like pressure, squeezing, or fullness. the pain is most often in the center of the chest.It may also be felt in the jaw, shoulder, arms, back, and stomach. It can last for more than a few minutes, or come and go.
Mostly found Symptons:-
Cold sweat
Lightheadedness
Nausea (more common in women)
Numbness,aching, or tingling in the arm (Usually the left arm,but the right arm may be affected alone, or along with the left)
Shortness of breath
Weakness or fatigue, especially in older adults and in women
Do's:
Have the person sit down, rest, and try to keep calm
Loosen any tight clothing
Ask if the person takes any chest pain medicine, such as nitroglycerin, for a known heart condition, and help them take it
If the pain does not go away promptly with rest or within 3 minutes of taking nitroglycerin,call for emergency medical help
Have the person sit down, rest, and try to keep calm
If an infant or child is unconscious and unresponsive, perform 1 minute of CPR
Dont's:
Don't leave the person alone except to call for help, if necessary
Don't allow the person to deny the symptoms and convince you not to call for emergency help
Don't wait to see if the symptoms go away
Don't give the person anything by mouth unless a heart medicine (such as nitroglycerin) has been prescribed
